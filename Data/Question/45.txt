Reading the blogs of soldiers on active duty in Iraq provides a fascinating glimpse of life in the middle of a war zone. (1) One day, while driving a military truck, the blog's author accidentally damages an old Kurdish man's car. (2) More astonishingly, he tells the old man that he will see that the car is repaired. He then leaves, promising to return in a week. (3) As an aside to the reader, the soldier happily mentions that the money is likely going to be enough to purchase a brand-new car. (4) The two men then chat for a while, discussing everything from politics to sports. They even show each other photos of their loved ones. As the soldier prepares to leave, the old man says, "(5)" The soldier is moved and in his blog he sums up the whole event by writing, "In the middle of this ghetto in some no-name neighborhood in the backwoods of a city in northern Iraq, two men met and became friends."
