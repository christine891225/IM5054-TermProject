(A) Exemptions to fasting include illness, old age, menstruation, pregnancy, breast-feeding and travel.(B) The act of fasting is said to redirect the heart away from worldly activities, its purpose being to cleanse the soul by freeing it from harmful impurities.(C) Actions of generosity and compulsory charity are encouraged during the period.(D) It takes place during the ninth month of the Muslim calendar and is considered the most sacred month of year by Muslims.(E) The Quran is guidance for mankind, the criterion of right and wrong and clear proof of the guidance.(F) Muslims spend each day in prayer and in the study of the Quran.
